Role

Disciplinary goals and methods

Naturalist

Construct species accounts from visual observations of an ecosystem

Biotic experimentalist

Extend species accounts through experimental manipulation of species populations within an ecosystem

Abiotic experimentalist

Extend species accounts through experimental manipulation of environmental variables within an ecosystem

Modeler

Construct a food web and habitat preferences for a community of species using species accounts as a resource

Threat analyst

Predict and observe the impact of potential perturbations of an ecosystem using food web and habitat preference chart as resources

Ecosystem manager/applied ecologist

Design and progressively refine an ecosystem to meet an objective outcome using food web and habitat preference chart as resources

 

Role

Classroom activities

Naturalist

In groups assigned to ecosystems, students observe the ecosystems and record their observations about the species using a species notes tool on a shared (group) tablet (including photographs and screen snapshots). They share their species note by carrying their tablets to the associated species place or species tokens and updating (synchronizing), where species accounts are maintained. During this process, they can compare their observations with those of other groups who are also contributing to the species account.

Biotic experimentalist

In groups assigned to ecosystems, students address a community-constructed question about species relationships by designing, conducting, and reporting on experimental manipulations of species populations within that ecosystem. They attach copies of their experimental reports to their species notes, and synchronize them with the community species accounts.

Abiotic experimentalist

In groups assigned to ecosystems, students address a community-constructed question about species habitats by designing, conducting, and reporting on experimental manipulations of environmental conditions within that ecosystem. They attach copies of their experimental reports to their species notes, and synchronize them with the community species accounts.

Modeler

In whole class setting, students construct food web and habitat preferences models characterizing their collective community of species. Students report/propose (and support) additions or modifications to the model. Class reaches consensus on changes to master food web and/or posts questions on the question board. Questions are assigned to each student group, which become the driver for the biotic and abiotic experimentalist activities reported at next modeling session. Modeling work is done daily in conjunction with Naturalist and Experimentalist activities, and as needed during Threat analyst and Ecosystem manager activities.

Threat analyst

On group tablets, students prepare analyses preparatory to two potential threats (invasive species and temperature change). Teacher activates threats. Students evaluate their predictions and revise theories. May be repeated for additional threats. May involve model revision.

Ecosystem manager/applied ecologist

Students adopt design objective and prepare a set of ecosystem transformations designed to achieve that objective. All work done using regular classroom tools.
